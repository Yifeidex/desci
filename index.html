The pharma industry relies on patents to charge high prices, making drug development slow and costly. Off-patent drugs could be repurposed faster and cheaper, but without the ability to charge monopoly prices, they don't get funded. This system hinders medical innovation.
Over 20,000 off-patent drugs and nutraceuticals could be repurposed to treat new diseases for 1/100th of the cost and 5-10x faster than new patented drugs.
Off-patent treatments struggle to secure private funding for Phase II/III trials, as they can't charge monopoly prices. Clinical trials for these drugs can cost $1-20 million, with a 50-75% risk of failure.
